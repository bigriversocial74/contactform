# Enterprise Local Impact Model v1

## Purpose

Enterprise profiles represent companies, sponsors, employers, and large organizations that stimulate local demand through programs, rewards, incentives, and local purchasing activity.

A local profile asks how much future demand it can reasonably attract. An enterprise profile asks how much local activity it is helping stimulate by region and category.

## Core concepts

### Local Demand Commitment

Purchasing power, reward value, gift value, incentive value, or program funding committed toward local profiles, products, categories, or regions.

### Projected Local Demand Activity

A non-guaranteed projection based on committed demand, redemption behavior, repeat activity, merchant participation, and category or regional strength.

### Demand Multiplier

A platform-calculated multiplier that estimates how committed demand may activate additional local activity.

Example:

Committed local demand: 10000
Demand multiplier: 2.5
Projected local demand activity: 25000

This is a platform projection, not a guaranteed outcome.

## Enterprise profile metrics

Enterprise profiles can eventually show:

- active regions
- active categories
- participating local profiles
- participating employees, customers, or members
- local demand events
- committed local demand
- projected local demand activity
- average demand multiplier
- claim rate
- redemption rate
- repeat activity rate
- top local categories
- top local regions

## Regional and category views

Microgifter should eventually measure demand activity by city, ZIP code, neighborhood, region, category, merchant type, creator type, profile type, enterprise program, campaign, and time period.

## Program examples

Enterprise local activity programs may include employee rewards, workplace gifting, customer incentives, sales contests, wellness benefits, local merchant support campaigns, creator sponsorships, nonprofit support programs, and event or venue activation.

## Stage boundary

Stage 6C defines the model and language only.

Later implementation belongs to:

- Stage 14 for content, reviews, public activity, and social signals
- Stage 15 for committed demand, PSR, future visits, demand velocity, regional metrics, and category metrics
- Stage 16 for agent recommendations and approval-gated demand actions
- Stage 17 for admin, moderation, dispute, and program oversight
- Stage 18 for jobs, snapshots, monitoring, queues, backup, and replay

## Guardrails

Enterprise local impact metrics should use measured platform behavior and clearly identified projections. Avoid language that implies guaranteed returns, guaranteed economic outcomes, or securities-style performance.
