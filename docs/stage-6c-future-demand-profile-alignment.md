# Stage 6C Future Demand Profile Alignment

## Purpose

Stage 6C is a planning and alignment stage. It connects the current Stage 6 customer-commerce work to the larger 18-stage Microgifter build plan without adding schema, backend scoring, or UI rebuilds ahead of schedule.

The core evolved concept is the Future Demand Profile.

A Future Demand Profile represents the demand capacity of a person, business, creator, artist, musician, organization, venue, nonprofit, brand, local merchant, or enterprise sponsor.

## Core question

For profiles that attract demand:

How much future demand can this profile reasonably attract?

For enterprise sponsors:

How much local economic activity is this enterprise helping stimulate, and what future demand commitment is being created regionally and categorically?

## Build-plan alignment

This concept spans multiple roadmap stages:

- Stage 2: public profile placeholders and creator or merchant mode foundations
- Stage 8: owned items, entitlements, customer library, and downloads
- Stage 11: inbox and post-purchase action center
- Stage 14: posts, feed, social, reviews, and content activity
- Stage 15: PSR, committed demand, future visits, demand velocity, and profile demand signals
- Stage 16: agent commerce signals, recommendations, draft actions, and approval gates
- Stage 17: operations, moderation, fraud, disputes, and admin oversight
- Stage 18: reliability, queues, workers, webhooks, monitoring, backups, and snapshots

Stage 6C does not move the real Future Demand Intelligence engine forward into Stage 6. It creates the shared language and planning boundary so future work does not drift.

## Platform thesis

Microgifter creates Future Demand Profiles for people, businesses, creators, organizations, and enterprise sponsors by turning purchases, products, digital assets, reviews, content, locations, claims, redemptions, interactions, and local investment programs into measurable demand signals.

## Demand attraction and demand stimulation

Future Demand Intelligence has two sides:

1. Demand attraction: how much future demand a profile can reasonably attract.
2. Demand stimulation: how much demand a sponsor, program, or enterprise can activate in a local market.

Together:

Demand attraction + demand stimulation = Future Demand Intelligence.

## Language guardrails

Use demand, commitment, local impact, and projected activity language. Avoid implying guaranteed investment returns, securities, or guaranteed economic outcomes.

Preferred terms:

- Future Demand Profile
- Future Demand Signals
- Demand Readiness
- Committed Demand
- Future Demand Pipeline
- Local Demand Commitment
- Projected Local Demand Activity
- Demand Multiplier
- Enterprise Local Impact

Avoid using investment language as the primary product label unless the context is clearly non-securities local spend, sponsorship, rewards, or purchasing commitment.

## Stage 6C outcome

Stage 6C should produce documentation only:

- Future Demand Profile alignment
- profile entity types
- demand signal map
- enterprise local impact model

No schema changes, no backend scoring engine, no permanent workflow, and no UI rebuild are introduced in this phase.
